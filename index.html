Theoretical Distribution
Intro
Frequency distribution is a sample, a representative part of a large
Theoretical Probability Distribution means assigning different probabilities discrete (Binomial/Poisson) or continuous variables(Normal)
May be profitably employed to make short term projections for the future
Statistical analysis only possible basis on TPD, setting confidence limits or testing statistical hypothesis about population parameter(s) is based on the probability distribution of the population under consideration
Binomial Distribution
Intro
Most important and frequently used
derived from Bernoulli process..
known as symmetric when p = 0.5
Characteristics
Two mutually exclusive and exhaustive outcomes
One is success another failure
Trials are independent
Probability of success = p, failure = q = 1 - p
No. of trials is finite positive integer.
Important Points
n > 0, p,q >= 0. it follows that f(x) >= 0 for every x
known as biparametric distribution as it is characterised by n and p, if n and p are known then it is completely known
mean = np
may be unimodal, bi-modal. Mode is given by u = largest integer contained in (n+1)p
variance(Square of SD) = npq
p and q are less than or equal to 1, npq < np (variance is always less than mean
variance attains its maximum value when p = q = 0.5 and this maximum value is n/4
Additive property = X ~ B(N1,P) and Y ~ B(N21, P) then (X+Y) ~ B(n1 +n2, P)
Applications
When trails are independent
Each trial has just two outcomes success and failure
It is applied in coin tossing experiments, sampling inspection plan and genetic experiments
Poisson Distribution
Conditions
Prob of finding success in a very small time interval is kt, here K (>0) is a constant
Prob of having more than one success in this time interval is very low
Prob of having success in this time interval is independent of t as well as earlier successes
Points
X ~ P(m) . M is the variable.
Uniparametric distribution as only one variable, m.
Mean = m
Variance(Square of SD) = m
Could be also unimodal or bimodal
Bi if integer, Uni is non-integer m
if n, of a binomial distribution tends to infinity and p = zero. M = np remains finite.
when n is rather large and p is rather small, so that m = np is moderate then B (n,p) =~ P(m).
Additive Property: if X ~ P(m1), Y ~ P(m2). Z = X + Y ~ (m1 + m2)
Applications
When total no of events is large but probability of occurence is rather small.
No of printing mistakes per page of a large book
No of road accidents on a busy road per minute
No of radio-active elements per minute in a fusion process
No of demands per minute for health centre and so on
Normal Distribution
Points
defined by U and variance
X ~ N (u , variance)
u and variance are constants and variance is not zero
Normal Curve when we plot y = f(x)
bell shaped, one peak which means one mode
line drawn through mid point is x = U
divides the curve into 2 equal parts, known as symmetrical curve
ND is symmetrical when x = u (line dividing the curve is denoted by x and when it is u meaning it is mean/mode/median)
Two tails extend indefinitely and never touch the horizon
Total area for any prob curve = 1
Standard normal variate = z.
O (x) is known as cumulative distribution function
Properties
Mean = u, and since distribution is symmetrical when x = u.. Means that mean median and mode are equal
SD = Under root of variance
MD = 0.8 x SD
Q1 = u - 0.675 x SD
Q3 = u + 0.675 x SD
QD = 0.675 x SD
Two points of inflexion, i.e. when it changes its curvature from Concave to convex or vice versa.
First one is u - SD (When z = 01)
Second one is u + SD (When z = 1)
99.73 values lie b/w u - 3SD and u + 3SD. 0.0027 lie outside
Additive property: X ~ N (u1, v1) and Y ~ N(u2,v2) then X + Y ~ N(u1 + u2, v1 +v2)
Applications
Most continuous variables like height, weight, age follow normal distributions
If a variable does not follow normal distribution then simple transformation can lead it to follow normal distribution
If in Binomial Dist. , N = large and P = moderate, then the BD is also Normal Distribution
In Poisson also for large value of m approaches ND.
Such transformations become necessary as it is easier to compute probabilities under the assumption of a ND
Prob dist. of t, Chi-square and F also tend to ND under certain conditions
Standard Normal Distribution
Intro
If a continuous random variable z follows ND, Z ~ N(0,1)
Properties of z
Z has Mean median and mode all equal to zero
SD of Z is 1 and approx. values of MD and QD are 0.8 and 0.675
SND is symmetrical about z = 0.
Two points of inflexion are -1 and 0
Two tails never touch the horizontal axis
